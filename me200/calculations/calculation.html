Heat Rate Calculation
The values we got for the heat and work rates are consistent with the data on the CERV 2. The compressor’s datasheet gives values between 69 to 165 W, meaning that our calculation is accurate. The CERV 2 advertises a cooling rate of 1 to 2 kW; according to our calculations, the evaporator is absorbing 1.168 kW--this is the cooling rate that we can compare to the CERV 2’s advertised values.
Heat Rate Calculation(Convection)
Looking at the final values, there are a lot of questions that come up; first of all, these values are extremely high compared to what we obtained using the ideal cycle. With the air temperatures we assumed, the evaporator and condenser temperatures are likely not what we assumed them to be. One indication of this is that the condenser would be absorbing heat opposed to releasing it in recirculation--this is against the purpose of the condenser. The net heat for both modes are also the same, which is not expected considering the change in temperature gradient for the evaporator and condenser. Overall, the biggest issue with these calculations arises from the assumption of temperatures, which has the largest impact on these values. Convection heat transfer is a complex subject, which qualitatively helps us explain why the difference in cooling potential between ventilation and recirculation modes is so great, but is unable to accurately explain this quantitatively.
